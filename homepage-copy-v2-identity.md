# Homepage Copy — Version B: "Built for the Boots on the Ground"

**Angle:** Identity-first (founder authenticity + speed). Lead with "this is YOUR tool, built by someone who gets it" and the visceral speed of the product. Less pain-dwelling, more empowerment.

**Why this angle:** Some site managers aren't actively frustrated — they've accepted the chaos as normal. This version doesn't start with pain. It starts with a bold identity claim that makes them think "finally, someone built something for ME" and then proves it with speed.

## Problem / Solution Section

### Section Header:
Construction sites are messy. Your tools shouldn't be.

### The Problem:

**Three tools. None of them built for you.**

WhatsApp — great for chatting, terrible for tracking. Spreadsheets — fine at your desk, useless on a scaffold. Enterprise software — built for the IT department, not the person holding the torch in a basement.

You end up running projects across all three. Photos in one place. Notes in another. Reports cobbled together at home on a Sunday.

None of these tools were built by someone who's actually stood on a site at 7am in the rain.

### The Solution:

**One app. Built on real sites. Used with one hand.**

Snaglist does three things and does them fast:

1. **Capture** — Photograph a defect, mark it up, assign it to a trade. Five seconds.
2. **Share** — Send your contractor a Magic Link. They see exactly what to fix. No app. No login.
3. **Report** — Generate a branded PDF for your client. One tap.

That's it. No project management suite. No Gantt charts. No features you'll never touch. Just the fastest way to get snags captured, assigned, and closed.

> **Annotation:** The problem framing is different from V1 — it's not "WhatsApp is chaotic" but "nothing was built for you." The three-tool critique positions Snaglist as the first tool that understands site work. The solution mirrors the "three things" structure and ends with an anti-feature statement that builds trust.

## Key Features / Benefits

### Section Header:
The simplest tool in the shed.

### Feature 1: One-Handed Capture
**Headline:** Designed for site managers, not software demos.
**Body:** Photo, markup, location, trade assignment — one flow, five seconds. Works one-handed. Works offline. Works in the basement where nothing else does.

### Feature 2: Magic Links
**Headline:** Contractors click a link. That's the onboarding.
**Body:** No app to download. No account to create. No password to forget. Send your subbie a Magic Link and they see their snags — annotated photos, exact locations, clear descriptions. If they can open a link, they can use Snaglist.

### Feature 3: Instant PDF Reports
**Headline:** Client-ready reports from the front seat of your van.
**Body:** One tap turns your snag list into a professional, branded PDF. Every photo, every status update, every note. Send it to your client before you've left site.

### Feature 4: Track Without Chasing
**Headline:** See progress. Don't chase it.
**Body:** Every snag has a status: Open, In Progress, Resolved, Verified, Closed. Updated in real time. No more ringing around asking "is that done yet?"

### Feature 5: Floor Plan Pins *(Coming Soon)*
**Headline:** Show them where. Don't tell them where.
**Body:** Upload your drawings. Tap to place a pin exactly where the snag is. No more "it's in the second bedroom, left-hand wall, above the socket." A pin on a plan says it all.

> **Annotation:** V2 includes five features vs V1's four — the identity angle earns more patience for feature detail because the reader is already bought in on "this is for me." Each headline is a short, punchy reframe. "Front seat of your van" and "basement where nothing else does" ground the copy in site reality.
